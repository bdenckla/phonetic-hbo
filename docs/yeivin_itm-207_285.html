The Repetition of the Pashṭa Sign

239

# Pashṭa is the only accent sign in standard Tiberian MSS which is regularly repeated on a penultimate stress syllable. In standard printed editions, the pashṭa sign is repeated on every word in which the stressed vowel is not the last (i.e. every word with penultimate stress, including words with pataḥ “furtive”) Most early MSS - for instance L and C - follow the same system, but some MSS show a different convention.

In A, S, and some other MSS, the sign is only repeated where at least one letter stands between the two letters to be marked with the signs, as א and ב. Where this is not the case, the pashṭa sign is not repeated, as ג, ד, ד, and ד. This system of marking pashṭa is mentioned in some treatises, such as Qimḥi’s ʿEt Sofer, p. 31b:

When the two letters (on which the pashṭa signs would be marked) are separated only by one vowel, with no vowel letter, there is a difference of opinion. Some read two pashṭa signs, others only one, as in א.

In B and S1, and some other MSS, pashṭa is not repeated not only where two letters on wich it would be marked are not separated by a third, but also in other situations as well, as ג (μB) and ד (μB). This usage is not consistent, however, as pashṭa sometimes is repeated on such words.

In μL2, pashṭa is never repeated. Thus א and ב.

In some cases, where the position of the word stress might be in doubt, the pashṭa sign is repeated on a stressed final syllable, as א (μC), ד (μC, μS1), and ד (μS1).

In many MSS pointed in the expanded Tiberian system, pashṭa is repeated on every word in which the last letter does not represent the first consonant of the stress syllable, as א, ב, and ג.
