1. In Jacobson, this word is pointed שֶׁשֱּׁזָפַ֖תְנִי, i.e. with a ḥataf segol under the second shin. In my opinion this is excessive adherence to ל because ל is anomalous here. No reader-friendly edition will be pointed that way.

2. In Jacobson, this word is pointed וְהַֽלְוִיִּם֒, i.e. with a meteg on הַ, . MAM lacks such a meteg not only in this location (2Chr. 24:5) but in all locations where words ending in לְוִיִּם appear.

3. In Jacobson, this word is pointed מִֽמָּ֣חֳרָ֔ת, i.e. with a munaḥ under the second mem. Jacobson does not provide a reference locale for this word and I cannot find one with this particular munaḥ pointing. In Jacobson this word is pointed with a qamats (ambiguous as to gadol/qatan) under the second mem. This pointing is expected since in Jacobson the qamats mark is always intentionally ambiguous. And anyway here Jacobson is highlighting a dispute about the “quality” (gadol/qatan) of this particular qamats. In the theoretical (ד) variant of MAM, this mem is pointed with qamats qatan. This word is is discussed in the body text as if it had already been mentioned (e.g. as if it had already appeared in the table above) but it has not already been mentioned.
