Dagesh and Rafe

Dagesh (and Rafe) Indicating that a Letter Represents a Consonant (or a vowel)

394

Dagesh and rafe may be used to show whether the letters א, ה, or ו represent consonants or vowels.

(i) He representing a consonant at the end of a word is marked by a dot (identical in form to dagesh) called mappiq. Non-consonantal final he is generally (but not always) marked with rafe. In some manuscripts consonantal he may be marked with mappiq even within a word, especially where it is pointed with shewa as
